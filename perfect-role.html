Think there’s only a handful of job opportunities in the travel industry? Well think again, with the help of our handy A-to-Z guide

A - C

Represent a company or clients to grow awareness and sales in the travel industry

Creative and business-focused roles booking and developing TV, newspaper or online campaigns for a travel company or in an advertising agency

Pilots and cabin crew staff the aircraft, supported by colleagues in customer service, sales, technology, engineering and manufacturing

Security guards, special assistance teams, baggage handlers and ground staff work with senior managers to keep airports working efficiently

Membership organisations represent specific travel sectors, offering training, consumer guidance and government lobbying

Arrange business trips from within a company’s travel department or an external travel agency

Sell holidays by phone or online and deal with customer enquiries or feedback after a booking is made

Work directly with guests taking bookings or running activities, or behind the scenes in maintaining the site and its facilities

Help get travellers where they need to go with jobs in sales, administration, accounting, marketing and servicing vehicles worldwide

Drivers and tour guides help run holidays by coach, as well as reservations staff, operations managers or itinerary planners

Work with hotels and resorts around the world to organise big events and exhibitions that bring business people together

Create inspiring videos, photos or stories that showcase a destination or brand for use on social media, websites, marketing and brochures

This dynamic sector includes a variety of sales, commerce and management positions as well as jobs on board, from entertaining guests as cruise director to captaining the ship

D - G

Ranging from software developers to content producers, companies use desktop and mobile technology to reach consumers and manage bookings

Make online transactions smoother and simpler from start to finish in this developing area of digital technology

Add-on providers look after the extras that help make a holiday happen, including airport parking, transfer services and travel insurance

Ferry companies play a vital role in getting people and cargo from A to B, employing onboard engineers and crew along with operations managers and communications teams

As an account manager or business development manager, this role involves visiting travel agencies, holding in-person training or representing your brand at a conference

Travel companies rely on finance teams to manage cash flow, as well as staff salaries, audits and tax

Based in a travel agency or financial institution, foreign exchange specialists advise on overseas currency and new cashless payment cards for transactions abroad

Technology platforms connect airlines, hotels and transfer companies to the front-line agents who sell holidays

This wide-ranging area encompasses everything from guided tours to organising school trips and local clubs or groups travelling together

H - O

Oversee workplace training and check hotels and holiday suppliers are conforming to strict safety standards

A growing number of travel sellers work from home, dealing with clients by phone or online on behalf of a company or their own business

The sector employs millions across the world, with a host of customer-facing and back-office hospitality roles

Travel insurance is essential when things go wrong, with jobs in risk assessment, product development and client services

All industries rely on robust IT, from day-to-day support for remote working to developing more-efficient business systems

In-house lawyers, advisors and external firms work with travel companies to make sure they comply with relevant legislation

Creativity and commercial strategy come together in marketing departments to showcase and promote a brand’s unique selling points

Journalists work across newspapers, magazines, TV, radio and websites to report on travel, alongside sales, advertising and production teams

This catch-all term means making sure holidays run smoothly and seamlessly, and ensuring travel companies work efficiently

P - Y

Cruise and cargo ships call at passenger and container ports, where jobs range from logistics and operations management to safety and security

PRs are employed by travel companies, either in-house or via an agency, to communicate with media organisations and to generate creative content ideas

This area requires in-depth knowledge of a destination or sector to create interesting itineraries and holiday packages for travel companies to sell

Seek out the best suppliers, negotiate competitive rates and go through contracts in detail to make sure companies – and customers – get the best deal

From luxury train journeys to flight-free transport, rail holidays open the door to a variety of roles, from tour operators to technical positions

Holiday reps are employed by a tour operator but based in a big hotel or resort to help with customer enquiries, book transfers, arrange day trips and more

A vital team within a tour operator, cruise line or airline to take bookings, recommend suitable products and liaise with travel agents

Large hotels with wide-ranging facilities, where employees include housekeepers, restaurant and bar staff, groundskeepers, sports coaches and more

A specific role within web teams to optimise online content so your company or brand appears high in the results when potential customers search for a holiday

Skilled social media managers are increasingly needed to promote brands through clever marketing, maintain the right tone of voice and resolve complaints effectively

Work with children and teens in a summer camp overseas, with the US being a popular destination for this sector

Tourist attractions and cultural landmarks need people to welcome visitors, plan promotions or manage staff and sites

A service offering everything from skip-the-line passes and attraction tickets to rail or coach transport, either for consumers or companies

Knowledgeable experts who lead travellers around a destination, from short city tours to adventures in remote landscapes around the world

These companies arrange each element of a holiday, from flights and transfers to hotels and tours, offering a wealth of customer-facing and behind-the-scenes jobs

An essential link in the journey, transfer companies drive guests to and from airports, cruise ports or rail stations

Working on behalf of a city, region or country to promote its assets to consumers and companies, either in the UK or overseas

Staff training is key to achieving professional qualifications and ensuring compliance with ever-changing regulations

Knowledgeable and experienced travel advisors can recommend the best flights, hotels and tours to suit customers’ needs, and manage a booking from start to finish

Online platforms have never been more essential, meaning web designers and developers play a vital role in guiding the customer from initial interaction to point of sale

Sector in Focus: Aviation

The captain might be the one in the cockpit, but there are hundreds of different jobs involved in aviation, making sure every step of a passenger’s journey goes smoothly, from when they arrive at the airport to the moment they touch down in another country.

Check-in staff greet customers at the airport and manage the boarding process, while cabin crew often have the most interaction with the public as they look after passengers’ welfare while in the air – and occasionally get to stay and explore the destination before they fly back!

Yet like any large, multinational company, airlines employ thousands of people in their offices. They include IT developers who make complex websites as user-friendly as possible, training managers who ensure staff are up to speed on changing rules and regulations, marketers who design creative TV adverts and operations teams who manage the logistics of keeping a huge aircraft fleet up and running.

The UK’s busy airports have just as many wide-ranging roles, from staff in shops, restaurants or business-class lounges to air traffic control teams who guide pilots on and off the runway and highly trained engineers who oversee aircraft safety. Security guards and ground handling teams also work hard to keep passengers – and their luggage – flowing through the airport as efficiently as possible.

When it comes to a career as cabin crew, I am proof that the sky really is the limit!
